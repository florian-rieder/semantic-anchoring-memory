The novel "Nineteen Eighty-Four" was published on 8 June 1949 by Secker & Warburg.

The story is set in an imagined future in the year 1984, in a world perpetually at war.

The novel examines the role of truth and facts within societies and the ways in which they can be manipulated.

Airstrip One is the name of the province of the totalitarian superstate Oceania, where the story takes place.

The Party engages in omnipresent government surveillance, historical negationism, and constant propaganda to persecute individuality and independent thinking.
