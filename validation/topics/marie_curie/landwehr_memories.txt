Marie Curie was born on November 7, 1867 in Warsaw, Congress Poland, Russian Empire

She died on July 4, 1934 at the age of 66 in Passy, Haute-Savoie, France

Marie Curie was a citizen of Poland by birth and France by marriage

She studied at the University of Paris and ESPCI

Marie Curie is known for her pioneering research on radioactivity and the discovery of polonium and radium

She was married to Pierre Curie and had two daughters, Irène and Ève

Marie Curie won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903 and the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1911

She worked at the University of Paris, Institut du Radium, École Normale Supérieure, French Academy of Medicine, and International Committee on Intellectual Cooperation
