The Statue of Liberty is located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor in New York City.

The height of the copper statue to the torch is 151 feet 1 inch (46 meters), and from ground level to the torch is 305 feet 1 inch (93 meters).

The statue was dedicated on October 28, 1886.

The sculptor of the statue is Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi.

The statue receives 3.2 million visitors in 2009.

The governing body of the Statue of Liberty is the National Park Service.

The statue is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, designated in 1984.

It is also a U.S. National Monument, designated in 1924 by President Calvin Coolidge.

The Statue of Liberty is listed on the U.S. National Register of Historic Places, the New Jersey Register of Historic Places, the New York State Register of Historic Places, and is a New York City Landmark.
